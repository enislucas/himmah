▲ Read Me First

A friend sent you this. Here's everything you need, in order, to go from "unzipped it" to "using it every day" - what to click, what you should see, and what to do if something looks off. You don't need to know anything technical.

1. What is Himmah?

Himmah is a calm, private to-do dashboard: you type whatever's on your mind and it turns into a sorted list of tasks, habits and a weekly calendar built around your five daily prayers. It runs entirely on this computer - your data never leaves it unless you choose to switch on an optional AI helper later.

2. How to open it

Unzip it anywhere Right-click the ZIP you were sent → Extract All... (or use your favourite unzip tool) → pick any folder - your Desktop, Documents, anywhere you like.
You should see: a new folder called Himmah appears, containing Start Himmah.bat, index.html, a Setup Guides folder, and a data folder.
Double-click Start Himmah.bat Open the Himmah folder and double-click Start Himmah.bat. Here is exactly what it does, so nothing surprises you:
1. Opens a plain black console window titled "Himmah - keep this window open while you use the app".
2. Checks whether Python 3 is already on your PC. Himmah is built on it and needs it to run.
3. If Python is missing, it installs it FOR you automatically - no admin password needed - first trying Windows' own app installer (winget), then falling back to downloading the official installer from python.org and running it silently. You don't need to do anything for this except wait a minute.
4. After 3 seconds it opens your normal web browser at http://127.0.0.1:7777/ - that's Himmah, running only on your own PC (that address never leaves your computer).
You should see: the black console prints "Starting Himmah... it will open in your browser at http://127.0.0.1:7777/" and "Keep THIS window open while you use the app. Close it to stop Himmah." - then your browser opens showing the Himmah board.
If Windows shows a blue "Windows protected your PC" screen: this is normal for any script you got from a friend rather than the Microsoft Store - it isn't a virus warning about what's inside, just an "I don't recognise this publisher" flag. Click More info → Run anyway.
If a Windows Firewall box asks to allow Python/Himmah to communicate: click Allow access. It's safe - by default Himmah only listens on your own PC; allowing it just avoids any hiccups.
If the console says Python was installed but still can't find it: it will tell you to close the window and double-click Start Himmah.bat a second time - that one extra click lets Windows pick up the new install. If even that fails, it prints manual steps: go to python.org/downloads, click the big yellow Download Python button, run the installer and tick "Add python.exe to PATH", then try Start Himmah.bat again.

3. Make yourself a desktop shortcut (optional, 1 min)

Send it to your Desktop In the Himmah folder, right-click Start Himmah.bat → Send to → Desktop (create shortcut).
You should see: a new shortcut icon on your Desktop called "Start Himmah - Shortcut".
Rename it (and, optionally, set an icon) Right-click the new Desktop shortcut → Rename → type Himmah. That's all most people need.
You should see: the shortcut now reads simply "Himmah".
About a custom icon: this ZIP ships icon.svg, icon-192.png and icon-512.png (used inside the app/browser tab), but no .ico file - and Windows shortcuts can only pick up .ico files in the "Change Icon" dialog, not .png/.svg directly. Simplest: skip this, the default icon works fine. If you really want a custom one, convert icon-512.png to .ico with any free online converter first, then Properties → Change Icon → Browse to it.

4. Getting to fully working, in order

First open: explore the demo The very first time, Himmah opens with a filled-in demo week already inside it (a fictional person's tasks, habits and prayer times) so there's something real to look at immediately - not a blank page.
You should see: a populated board with categories, a calendar with events, and a few habits already tracked.
Follow the guided tour A tour starts on its own the first time, split into short chapters with a little map showing done/current/upcoming, so it never overwhelms you.
You should see: a dimmed overlay highlighting one part of the screen at a time, explaining what it does, with Next / Back buttons.
Want to skip it (now or partway through)? Click Skip tour (or "skip this chapter") any time - nothing is lost, you can always retake it from the tour button in the ✦ AI menu.
Switch on the AI (optional, recommended, ~2 min) Open the Setup Guides folder inside your Himmah folder and follow 1 - Switch on the AI (DeepSeek key, 2 min).html. It walks you through getting your own free/cheap DeepSeek key and pasting it in - step by step, nothing assumed.
You should see: once done, the ✦ AI button (top-left) shows "on" and features like ✦ Enhance actually rewrite your messy captures.
Don't want to bother? Totally fine - skip straight to the next step. See section 5 below for exactly what you'd be missing (it's small).
Import your prayer times into the Calendar Open Calendar (top bar) → click 💋 prayer times → click Populate calendar. It ships with a demo mosque's timetable already loaded, so this works immediately with no setup.
You should see: a row of 🏘 prayer blocks appear across the next 7 (or 30) days in your calendar.
Want YOUR real mosque's times instead of the demo ones? In that same popup, click Upload prayer times in your mosque (PDF) and pick your mosque's timetable PDF - it reads it in automatically.
When you're ready: clear the demo and make it yours Either finish the guided tour and click its last button, ☢ Nuclear - start MY journey, or anytime click ✦ AI (top-left) → 🌙 Make it yours. Either safely wipes the demo data and then walks you, chat-style, through building your own tasks, habits and rhythms.
You should see: the demo tasks disappear and a friendly chat window asks about your real life instead. Don't worry - the demo isn't gone forever: it's saved automatically in History (top bar → ⋮ More → History) if you ever want to peek at it again.
This part needs the AI key from step 3 above to have its smart conversation - without a key it still clears the demo but can't chat-build things for you (you'd add tasks/habits by hand instead, which works fine too).

5. What works without the AI key

You are never stuck. Everything in Himmah works fully offline and forever, with zero setup, except the handful of buttons marked ✦ (Enhance, Enrich, Triage, the Idea Parking Lot's Sort/Enhance, and the smart chat parts of Make it yours / Naseer). Without a key those just show a small "AI is off" message when clicked - harmless, nothing breaks, the rest of the app is completely unaffected. Specifically, without any key you still get: capturing thoughts, the whole board/calendar/habits/rhythms, dragging and organising tasks, the Guardian/Waiting/Tidy/Archive/History views, the entire guided tour, prayer-time importing (section 4 above), and even typing a deadline in plain English like "call the dentist tomorrow" or "in 3 days" or "next week" - that free date-recognition needs no AI at all. Even Calendarize tomorrow has a basic offline version that lays your day out without a key, just a simpler one than the AI version.

More detail any time: README.md in the Himmah folder, or the two guides in Setup Guides. Enjoy - and Bismillah on your own journey with it. ▲
